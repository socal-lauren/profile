Certificate authorities are well trusted entities that issue digital certificates which bind a public key and a named subject together. These certificates are usually requested by local applications and can be verified with a computationally infeasible operation. The security behind using certificate authorities as a means of preserving confidentiality, authenticity and integrity, lies behind the ability to breach the CA office or compromise the public key encryption method used. DigiNotar, a Dutch certificate authority, had their office breached in 2011, which caused them to file for bankruptcy. Most certificate authorities have high levels of security around their office, which is why it is still the preferred method of information security.
